About FinEval

Shared tasks are collaborative initiatives where researchers and practitioners work together to address a common challenge using shared datasets and evaluation metrics. FinEval — Financial Information Access and Evaluation — aims to foster competition while providing a community venue, with new editions called annually alongside the FinNLP workshop.

For 2026, the call for task proposals is now closed. The FinEval 2026 shared task is detailed below. Participants submit a system description paper (see the Call for Papers), published in the ACL Anthology and presented at the workshop. See also the official FinEval page.

Shared Task: Multilingual Financial Language Understanding

The FinEval 2026 shared task advances multilingual financial language understanding across three complementary phenomena: entity extraction in Greek, pragmatic intent recognition in Japanese, and cross-document multilingual financial question answering. Participants may take part in one, two, or all three subtasks.

Register
// Subtask 1

Greek Financial NER

Numeric and textual named-entity recognition over company filings from Athens Stock Exchange firms.

Data: GRFinNUM & GRFinNER 500 instances (320 / 80 / 100) Metric: Entity F1
// Subtask 2

Japanese Implicit Communication Recognition (JF-ICR)

Classify implicit intent — such as commitment, non-commitment, risk signaling, or refusal — in Japanese company Q&A.

94 expert-annotated JP Q&A instances Metric: Accuracy
// Subtask 3

Multilingual Financial Q&A

Cross-document multilingual financial QA combining SEC filing statements with news, based on MultiFinBen / PolyFiQA.

Data: PolyFiQA-Easy & PolyFiQA-Expert 172 QA examples EN · ZH · JA · ES · EL Metric: ROUGE-1

How to Participate

  • Register your team via the registration form.
  • Access data & baselines — the practice set, datasets, and starter kits are released to registered participants on the schedule below.
  • Submit predictions during the test phase, following the instructions shared with participants.
  • Write a system paper using the ACL template, published in the ACL Anthology.
  • Present in person at FinNLP @ EMNLP 2026 in Budapest.
